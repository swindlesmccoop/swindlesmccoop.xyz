lb

[link—standalone]

The first software I am going to review on this blog is the software that I actually use to make it, lb. The "software" is actually a <100 line shell script. I've looked at the entire thing and it's quite cool how it works. It uses sed and grep, and it's able to make an entire publication with just one HTML file. It updates your rss.xml, blog index, and year page (currently at 2021.html). The cool thing about it is how compact and neat it can create a blog. I could customize it, but I don't really want to. I kind of put my own dark theme on it as of the time of writing this, but that's really it, I like it's style really. It can do more in CSS than I can without referencing StackOverflow.

The author of the software is a person named Luke Smith. If you don't know about him, he makes lots of cool videos on YouTube, mostly about Linux, and he develops simple software for others to use, all while living in a cabin in the middle of the wilderness. He uses Artix Linux and he is generally very friendly towards beginners who have no clue what they're doing.

Enough about the author of the software though, let's talk usage!
lb is actually just a bash script that automate making posts to a blog that you can design yourself with CSS and HTML or just use a prestyled version (kind of what I use, though I have a few tweaks for readability). The great part about Linux is that you can just mark the script as executable and send it to /usr/bin and you're all good to go. You can easily create a new post just by cding into your site directory and running lb n, with the n standing for new. Then, it pulls up your favorite text editor (in my case Vim) and you make an HTML post. Afterwards, you type lb p intro your terminal and boom, it's ready. It automatically updates 2021.html, rss.xml, and it makes a separate page in the blog directory at the root of the site folder. Pretty cool, huh? Admittedly, like every person ever who has any knowledge of scripting, I've edited and tailored it to my own wants, such as permanently setting the directory of the blog, changing the CSS, etc.

If you want to make any kind of simple blog, I would tell you to go ahead with lb. It's simple and quick to learn the usage, it's powerful, it's easy, and best of all it really does all of the hard work for you.
